The 52 Million Project

Anyone can be a philanthropist.

Role VP of Design
Responsibilities UX/UI, Brand, Print, Photography, Video, Social
Organization The 52 Million Project
Location Columbus, Ohio
Overview

I was asked to be the first executive committee member of The 52 Million Project, a new nonprofit based in Columbus, Ohio. The mission is to educate people that anyone and everyone can be a philanthropist — even if it's donating just one dollar a week.

As Vice President of Design, my responsibilities span the full creative scope of the organization: UX/UI design, multimedia marketing materials, print collateral, photography, videography, and social media.

The landing page, designed to educate and convert first-time visitors.

The Challenge
01

Educating potential donors on The 52 Million Project and the importance of "secondary impact."

02

Increasing the number of donors from zero to one million.

Problem 01

Educating donors on secondary impact.

To get this nonprofit off the ground, we knew our website would be our best selling point to attract and educate potential donors. Our main goal was to deliver a high-quality experience that would be easy to use, communicate trustworthiness, and tell the story of our nonprofit.

Knowing that we would need to educate potential donors before converting them to a donation, we began with a competitive analysis of similar nonprofits to understand their process of user education.

Mind-mapping the most important aspects of The 52 Million Project helped us identify which pages would be most crucial to the education experience. From that map we built a user flow covering:

  • An educational, yet eye-catching landing page
  • An impact page showcasing donor count and the 3 main areas of giving
  • A dedicated page explaining the importance of "secondary impact"
  • A company origin story
  • A contact page for questions, volunteering, and social connection

Problem 02

Converting visitors into donors.

The main goal of The 52 Million Project is to have 1 million people donate $1 a week. With 0 donors and 52 million to go, the process of converting visitors had to be simple, quick, and painless.

To ensure users always knew how to donate, we created a persistent call-to-action present on every page — keeping users one click or tap away from the donation flow at all times.

Using prototypes I created in Adobe XD as a guide, the website was built in Webflow by our partner developer. Throughout the build, I worked closely with the developer — conducting periodic testing to confirm functionality and ensure the finished site stayed true to our vision and resembled the prototype as closely as possible.

Social Media

Building a brand presence.

Beyond the website, I developed a consistent visual language across all social media channels — creating graphics, photography direction, and templated posts that reinforced the mission and kept the brand recognizable across every touchpoint.
